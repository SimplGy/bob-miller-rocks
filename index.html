  • "The Grandfather"
    A poem about Bob Miller
    Written by his granddaughter Elizabeth Miller
    13 May 2015

    He has globe-colored eyes
    Faded at the edges
    Washed out by the sun like old blue jeans
    Diffused-edges:
    His irises are the sky
    Coming through the clouds
    Of memory
    Love
    Patience
    Gentleness
    Wisdom
    and humor.
    But most of all
    Time.

    I realize as I stare eye-to-eye
    That his eyes are exactly
    My father's.
    To see through my father to my grandfather,
    The same eyes in each,
    Is like turning deeper into the pages of history,
    And finding yourself.

    I am struck
    almost to tears
    By the ghostly life
    Of family,
    Past generations,
    Current generations,
    And future.
    The impossibility of knowing all,
    Or even remembering much.
    So much lost,
    So much unknown,
    So much beyond reach,
    Even as I gaze eye-to-eye,
    With this link to a past I have never known.
    And I realize that I can never know it,
    While time is slipping through the hourglass
    Too fast for me.

    I know my wealth,
    Even as I sense my loss,
    And I know I can never be grateful enough
    For this moment
    For this man
    For my family,
    Even those I do not know.
    The beloved strangers
    I try to learn.

    To hold it all
    Is to try and catch rain
    Wasting so much.
    As so many blinks
    Of tired blue eyes.

    I cannot help but think of my own:
    My numbered blinks
    What will I see?
    Who will look into my eyes?
    I cannot imagine
    That they will hold half as much
    As his.

    To love so deeply,
    So late,
    Is a gift:
    The epitome of life
    and time.

    ...

    To learn but one thing from him
    Is to let our eyes be globes
    Worlds to others
    Where we let them in
    To find that we are far bigger on the inside
    Than we can be on the outside.
    To let that value be discovered
    Patiently, over years.
    To always fully be there
    Until we are not.

    from Elizabeth Miller

  • I remember the last time I spoke to Dad was Father’s Day weekend with Eric and Scott. Dad had his sense of humor right up until the end. We told him we loved him and there was a pause due to the electronics and cell tower transmission. Dad said, “what did you have to do...vote on it,”

    from Shawn Miller

  • I remember how much Grandpa loved his hobbies, particularly sailing and golf. I started playing golf mostly to spend time together with the Miller men, but I still remember his lessons: The short game is more important than long drives. A wood can often be used to “putt” onto the green en lieu of a wedge. Watch professional women play for the best example of technique. Grandpa enjoyed sharing his knowledge about golf perhaps as much as playing it- and know that whenever I play, i’ll remember him.

    from Scott Miller

  • I remember that Scott and I always looked forward to picking a James Bond movie to watch. Grandpa had them all. An entire cabinet under the tv, full left-to-right of VHS tapes of every James Bond film ever made, perfectly aligned, with crisp cardboard sleeves in beautiful condition. Probably chronologically sorted too, though we wouldn't have recognized it at the time.

    from Eric Miller
